Key Considerations

When choosing a resin for relining, several factors influence the work time and overall curing process. Here's a breakdown of the key considerations:

1
Resin Volume

The volume of resin required is directly related to the length and diameter of the liner. Larger amounts of resin generate more heat during curing, which can significantly reduce the work time. This is due to the exothermic reaction that takes place as the resin hardens. In large volumes, heat builds up quickly, accelerating the curing process and reducing the time available to properly work with the material. To manage this, it's advisable to mix resin in smaller batches or use resins designed for longer pot life in high-volume applications.

2
Ambient Temperature

High temperatures in the surrounding environment will speed up the chemical reaction in the resin, shortening its work time. Conversely, colder temperatures can slow down the curing process. Therefore, you must adjust the type of resin and hardener based on the expected temperature at the installation site. For warmer climates, a slower-curing resin may be necessary, while faster-curing systems may be preferable in colder conditions.

3
Sun and Shade Exposure

Direct sunlight can significantly increase the ambient temperature around the liner, further accelerating the curing process and reducing the available working time. Working in shaded areas or during cooler parts of the day can help maintain a more controlled curing environment. If unavoidable, using marquees covering over the liner can mitigate this effect.

By considering these factors — resin volume, ambient temperature and sun exposure — you can choose a resin system that balances work time with optimal curing performance, ensuring a successful and durable pipe relining job.
